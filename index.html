Publications

Thesis
Real-time 2D manipulation of plausible 3D appearance using shading and geometry buffers

Middle-ground approach, between traditional artists paint and complex algorithms to create virtual scenes, for manipulating appearance. 3D-like manipulation tools performed on the 2D space based on statistical relationships between material/lighting and shading.

Local Shape Editing at the Compositing Stage

Tools to modify the rendered normal buffer that turns into automatic modifications of the diffuse and the reflection buffers providing a plausible result

Statistical Analysis of Bidirectional Reflectance Distribution Functions

Study of the resulting 2D functions of BRDFs when fixing the view direction. This so-called "BRDF slice" can be analyzed as a filter on the local environment.

MatCap Decomposition for Dynamic Appearance Manipulation

A MatCap is an images of a sphere that conveys a material appearance. Its decomposition into a new representation permits dynamic appearance manipulation.

Specular kurtosis and the perception of hazy gloss

Hazy gloss investigation using a two-layered glossy material model with two centered Gaussian-like lobes. A PCA of a perceptual study shows realtionships between perceptual dimension of gloss and the kurtosis of the specular lobe
